Beer Pieterjan Prakken

I am an interdisciplinary PhD candidate in philosophy at the University of Groningen, specializing in humor and play within politics. My research bridges qualitative and quantitative methods with philosophical, historical, and cultural perspectives to explore how humor functions as a political force — organizing, mobilizing, and radicalizing subjects in complex ways. I hold dual Master’s degrees in philosophy and history (cum laude). In addition to my main project, I conduct separate historical work on urban spaces and Black washerwomen in Antebellum New Orleans. I am also a predoctoral fellow at Yale University’s Center for Cultural Sociology and a guest researcher at Utrecht University. I actively engage with the public through essays, interviews, and presentations in outlets like Trouw and Volkskrant, and at international festivals.
